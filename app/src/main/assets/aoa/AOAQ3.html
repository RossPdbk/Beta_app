Q3. Explain Knapsack problem

  • We are given 'n' number of objects and a knapsack ( bag ). Each object has differect weight and cost.

  • The knapsack or bag has the capacity of 'm'. That is the bag can hold total weight of 'm'.

  • Our job is to fill the bag with those objects. But while doing so we should make sure that the total cost of all the objects must be as high as possible.

  • The objective is to maximise the total profit earned

Example
Let us consider that the capacity of the knapsack W = 60 and the list of provided items are shown in the following table −

Item
Profit
Weight
A 280 40
B 100 10
C 120 20
D 120 24

Step 1 : First we will add a column which will have the ratio of profit/weight

Item
Profit
Weight
Ratio(Profit/Weight)
A 280 40 7
B 100 10 10
C 120 20 6
D 120 24 5

Step 2 : Sort the table in decreasing order of Ratio.

Item
Profit
Weight
Ratio(Profit/Weight)
B 100 10 10
A 280 40 7
C 120 20 6
D 120 24 5

Step 3 : Now start adding the items in the bag strating with item having highest ratio.

Weight avaible=60 and Profit=0

Add item B , Weight avaible = 60 - 10 = 50 Profit=100

Add item A , Weight avaible = 50 - 40 = 10 Profit=100 + 280 = 380

Now, C is chosen as the next item. However, the whole item cannot be chosen as the remaining capacity of the knapsack is less than the weight of C.

Hence, fraction of C (i.e. (60 − 50)/20) is chosen.

Now, the capacity of the Knapsack is equal to the selected items. Hence, no more item can be selected.

The total weight of the selected items is
10 + 40 + 20 * (10/20) = 60

And the total profit is
100 + 280 + 120 * (10/20) = 380 + 60 = 440

This is the optimal solution. We cannot gain more profit selecting any different combination of items.
