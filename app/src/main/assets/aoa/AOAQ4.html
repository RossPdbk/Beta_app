Q4. Explain 0/1 Knapsack problem

The only difference between Knapsack and 0/1 Knapsack is that in 0/1 Knapsack we do not take fractional part of the item.
Either the item is taken as a whole or else it is not taken.

  • We are given 'n' number of objects and a knapsack ( bag ). Each object has differect weight and cost.

  • The knapsack or bag has the capacity of 'm'. That is the bag can hold total weight of 'm'.

  • Our job is to fill the bag with those objects. But while doing so we should make sure that the total cost of all the objects must be as high as possible.

  • The objective is to maximise the total profit earned

Now, D is chosen as the next item. Even here the remaining capacity of the knapsack is less than the weight of D.

Thus D is also rejected.

Total Profit = 380
This is the optimal solution. We cannot gain more profit selecting any different combination of items.
