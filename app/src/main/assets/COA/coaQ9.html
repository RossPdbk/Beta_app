Explain Addressing modes.

  • Immediate mode: In immediate addressing the operand is specified in the instruction itself.
    Example: MOV AL, 20H (move the data 20H into AL register)
  • Direct Mode: The address of a memory location is given explicitly in the instruction.
    Example: ADD AL,[0404] (add the contents of offset address 0404 to AL)
  • Register mode: In register addressing the operand is placed in one of 8 bit or 16 bit general purpose registers. The data is in the register that is specified by the instruction.
    Example: MOV AL,BL (move the contents of BL register to AL register)
  • Register Indirect mode:The effective address of operand is stored in register and the instruction conatins address of register conatining address of data .
    Example: MOV AX,[BX](move the contents of memory location s addressed by the register BX to the register AX)
  • Base addressing: The operand’s offset is sum of displacement and the contents of the base register BX or BP.
    Example: MOV AL,[BX+05]
  • Indexed addressing mode: The operand’s offset is the sum of the content of an index register SI or DI and displacement.
    Example: MOV AX, [SI +05]
  • Based Indexed Addressing: The operand’s offset is sum of the content of a base register BX or BP and an index register SI or DI.
    Example: ADD AX, [BX+SI]
  • Based Indexed plus displacement addressing mode: In this mode of addressing the operand’s offset is given by offset=[BX or BP]+[SI or DI]+displacement.
    Example: MOV AX, [BX+SI+05]
